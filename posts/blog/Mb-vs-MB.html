Why is my Download Speed so Much Slower than Speed Tests

04/08/2022

Description

Have you ever gawked at your internet service providers (ISP's) advertised download speed; performed a speed test on a website such as fast.com or speedtest.net and was thrilled to see your speed at 100Mbps; but then when you try to download anything off the internet or a game from Steam, the download seems to take so much longer than you expected for your 100Mbps internet? This is not because your web browser or such as Steam is being slow, but because of the deceiving way bits and bytes work.

Short Answer

Your speed test and internet service provider is recording in a different measurement unit to your download. Your speed test is being measured in Mbps, while your download is being measured in MBps (note the capitalized B). The difference in measurements is a scalar factor of 8.

Long Answer

Bits and Bytes are two different ways of measuring data size. Bits are measured in the unit bits or b and Bytes are measured in bytes or B. A bits is the individual binary representation of a single state. For instance, a bit can take the value of 0 or 1. While a Byte is an ordered collection of 8 bits. An example of an 8-bit byte can be 10110100.

This is why when internet service providers or internet speed tests promise 100Mbps internet, it is actually a tricky marketing tactic since most people can't differentiate between Mbps (mega bits per second) and MBps (mega bytes per second).

These speeds often conflict with download files from websites and Steam since they are measured in MBps not Mbps. Since there are 8 bits in a byte, by doing the conversion from MBps and Mbps, you will find that there is a factor of 8 between the two. Making your speed test represent 8 times more download speed than you will realistically be able to download.
